What On-Demand Service Apps are?

 

It literally means a middle party software that acts as a source of connection between customers and service providers. On-demand services in simple words – facilitate communication – in audible, visual or textual forms – to get jobs done successfully.

Popular examples are Uber for taxis, Zomato for food, and Airbnb for hotels etc.

On-demand service platforms allow users to solve their daily problems. And in return, users are increasingly happy to pay for the services they get online. In this way, companies or on demand service providers grow their business reach and sales, and their customers find a convenient way of getting those services.

Why people use on demand delivery apps and what do they do on it:

 

Reason for the popularity of these apps is the ‘instant problem solving value’ users get. So how do on-demand services actually turn inconveniences into solutions in just a few taps?

  • “Oh no! I’ll be late, as it’s gonna take long for me to reach the bus stop. How about calling an Uber taxi to my current location – that’ll save me the time and effort”.
  • If I just order pizzas via Zomato app – I’ll be able to spend my full time with guests who have arrived already”.
  • “Now my inflexible job hours won’t hinder me from getting my father a regular medical check-up with Doctor on Demand app”.

Above are just a few daily-life scenarios to quote.

Apart from getting the service, users also:

  • Pay for services in the apps
  • Get real time notifications of service provider’s GPS location, time required, and audible, textual or even video communication and vice versa.
  • 70% of the users use on-demand platforms to search for hiring services online, while 62% of them use on-demand apps to check the live status of the service they’ve ordered.

 

Types and Nature of On-Demand Service Apps:

 

We can distinguish on-demand delivery apps into three forms depending on the nature of people involved:

  • P2P (Person To Person): These on-demand service platforms allow individual users to exchange products with users.
  • Examples: Olx and eBay
  • B2B (Business To Business): These on-demand delivery apps are solely for supporting communication and service exchange between businesses.
  • Examples: Cargomatic and Catalant
  • B2P (Business To Person): B2P or B2C on-demand delivery apps facilitate services and products delivery from companies to customers. Meaning companies direct their employees to reach out and provide products and services to its customers and make money with it.
  • Examples: Uber and Booking.com

 

How Do On-Demand Service Apps Work? General Steps:

 

1- User places a request.

2- A service provider sees the request.

3- Provider accepts or declines the request (depending on the situation).

4- System shows payment for authorization.

5- Provider delivers the service or product after payment authorization.

6- Customer confirms the service delivery.

7- Payment then gets deducted.

Now If You’ve Decided To Make an On-demand App – See These Must Consider Prerequisite Steps You Can’t Afford to Miss:

Do a Market Research

Competitors:

Collect data and try to understand the various strategies that your competitors have applied in their business. Look out for all the weaknesses and mistakes your rivals made in their journey – so that you’re not doing any of that.

You can then come up with better strategies to avoid any grave losses and grow your business fast.

Understand Your Target Audience:

Your app’s success purely depends on this factor. Survey your audience and see how their behavior patterns revolve around how they would like an on-demand app to be like. What is it that they don’t find attractive or questions like what incentivizes them to happily use an on-demand service?

Analyze their real pain points and question them on what they really want. By doing this, you’ll be able to devise clear goals and strategies accordingly.

All this will help you to know what is popular and what is not. So then you can take a good decision about what kind of app and what service you’d seek to provide.

Choose the Optimal Features:

If you’re launching a new app and want to know its viability as early as possible – then go for an MVP (Minimum Viable Product) to just add the most important features and have a safe start.

Some not to be missed out features include:

  • Convenient Registration Method:

Registration is necessary for user profiles, ratings, personalized journey experience etc. It’s thus imperative to add an easy and straightforward registration login method – with any social media account or email address.

  • Tracking / Geolocation

The geographical closeness of a service provider on the map is essential for saving both the parties some valuable time. Especially in delivery and transportation services it’s a necessity to specify pickup locations and destinations. Watching your taxi coming on a screen map is a very interactive experience and the customers simply love it.

           Source 

  • Notifications

Push notices play a great role in customer experience. Notify the users regarding payments, ratings, and other essential things that sum up to a complete customer journey.

  • Reliable and Easy Payments

People tend to super conscious when there is anything that involves their money or identity. So your payment system should be transparent and as secure as you can make it – to develop trust in your services. The best way is to use online financial payment services that are already reputable – like paypal.

  • Feed back

This is simply the rates and reviews of your customers about your service. This also develops an understanding in your customers – about the fact that you care.

  • Help & Support

Who can ever leave this portion when they are providing real time on-demand services online. This feature assists customers in case of any queries. This in turn, adds to the company’s reputation.

]]> We all know trends can be flaky and nowhere it’s more apparent than web design. What is popular today might not be popular tomorrow. The tides will continue to shift.

Fortunately, there are a few things you can foretell based on where most websites are headed. And if you can successfully do so, you can crush your competition before they even know what hit them.

Gathered are top five innovative web design trends that your online business or web design agency can follow in 2019:

The Rise of Playful Cursors

The traditional cursor serves just one purpose; get you where you want to go in a blink of an eye with a simple click. For years, it remained a disregarded element on websites because changing it seemed unnecessary to all those providing web design services given that it serves no aesthetic function.

But times are changing. Now more and more websites are opting for interesting and interactive designs, which has opened a new pathway for cursors too.

Most of all, the aesthetics of the cursor are rapidly changing and most of the time it syncs with the style of the web in question. For instance, if it’s a site about photography, your web design agency might turn the cursor into the center dot of a camera, so you can use it to snap the ‘pictures’ of different parts of the site you want to access.

This means gone are the days of just pointing and clicking. These days, you can even click and hold certain elements to get a video to play or a GIF to load on modern websites.

In fact, you get to see different functions based on whether you hold or click the mouse, giving your web development partner more room to play with interactive parts.

Screen Dominating Text Set to Rule the Web

Source

In 2019, text in web design will continue to thrive under the content first approach. For years, content specialists have argued that content should be at the heart of your strategy when it comes to publishing.

Sure, it seemed obvious in theory, but in practice, people providing web design services failed to pay heed to this aspect for years. Despite the fact that it’s one of the most important parts of the website.

Fortunately, we’ve stepped in the era where content is pushed to the forefront once again. Companies offering top software development companies are now reintroduced to the letterforms and websites are giving text center stage.

This welcome change is represented with huge letters on the home page of many websites. Huge fragmented textual messages on web pages instantly grab visitors’ attention, which perfectly serves the purpose of the message being displayed.

Whether it’s a worthwhile message or not, it still makes the visitors feel like it’s something urgent. At times the digital marketing agencies intentionally keep the font too large just to add an additional level of urgency.

Impactful Black & White With a Cherry on Top

 Source

Colors are something that can change the way people see your website. That’s why picking the right hues for your site is one of the most important things to consider.

People associate different moods with different colors and designers have taken advantage of this since the dawn of the internet. Though getting rid of most colors doesn’t seem like an effective idea, designers are becoming creative with the two most simple colors—black and white.

The two colors have always remained an interesting pair of art. One reflects openness and light, while the other is the absolute opposite, makes you feel imprisoned. Modern websites are leveraging these colors because they feel more professional and also allow the designers to show off their skills.

Lately, this trend has seen a surge, but this time around your digital marketing agency might use them with a minimum of other colors to emphasize something important. For example, gentle shade of red behind an element sticks out like a cherry on top.

Most importantly, using black and white with other colors allows you to grasp the attention of your users the way you like while maintaining the aesthetics.

Helvetica to Dominate Logo Designs

Source

With websites getting more vibrant and intriguing over time, the same can’t be said about website logos. The common logo is now given a new face-lift.

Last year, we witnessed an increase in playful wordmarks to represent the eccentric side of the brands. But this time, they are looking to achieve the minimalistic look of sans-serifs or similar styles. In a few words, every popular brand you see is now getting a Helvetica version of their logo.

While some brands may benefit from this simple approach, others might not be appreciated as much. Because this kind of cold design starts to annoy some people.

This might be a symbol of maturity and growth, but the lack of eccentricity will certainly remove the warmth from certain logos.
